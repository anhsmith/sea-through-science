\secmark{Profile}

I’m a statistician specialising in ecology and conservation, with more than 20 years’ experience as a scientist and educator. Before founding \textbf{Sea Through Science}, I was a Senior Lecturer in Statistics at Massey University, where I taught data analysis to students in ecology, biology, and conservation. I now work full-time as a consultant for the life sciences and maintain a strong relationship with \textbf{PRIMER-e}, providing statistical consulting and teaching international workshops in multivariate analysis for ecological data.

\medskip

Over the years, I’ve met \textbf{thousands of datasets}—each with its own quirks, limitations, and lessons. I’ve worked across every stage of ecological science, from \textit{design and data collection} to \textit{analysis, interpretation, and communication}. That perspective helps me set projects up well from the start, avoiding downstream pitfalls and getting the most value and insight from the data.

\medskip

My approach emphasises \textbf{clarity and collaboration}. I work closely with clients to define goals and success criteria, then deliver results—models, figures, and written outputs—that are accessible, decision-focused, and defensible.

\secmark{Selected expertise}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Bayesian modelling; generalised linear and mixed models
  \item Multivariate analysis (ordination, PERMANOVA, designs)
  \item Experimental and sampling design; power and precision analysis
  \item Reproducible workflows (Quarto/R), custom tools and reporting
\end{itemize}

\secmark{Applied domains}
Clinical and veterinary science; bioinformatics and ‘omics; fisheries and wildlife monitoring; conservation management; community ecology; water quality and environmental impacts.
